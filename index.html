Overview

A View of Catholic Feminism
in Chile, Kenya, and Spain

Welcome to my website! By choosing articles with stories of women’s experiences in Catholicism throughout a variety of places in the world, I want to give users as in depth of an understanding of Catholic Feminism as possible. My hope is to not only highlight the limitations the Catholic Church creates for women, but the ways in which women have defied the odds and broke the walls of gender norms and expectations in a seemingly static and unchanging religious community in terms of women’s role in the Church. Overall, my website will give an analysis of the three sources I have chosen for this project in a clear and easily-digestible format for students’ further education about the global intersection of religion and sexuality.

    Terms for Context

    Feminism

    Movement advocating for the recognition, equality, and empowerment of women while challenging gender hierarchies and cultural norms.

    Spirituality

    Response of our whole being towards what one perceives to be sacred; a deeply personal pursuit of meaning within the sacred.

    Kyriarchy

    Rule of the dominant, can include any race, gender, economic class, position of power; allows for intersectionality

    Androcentrism

    The interior/mental manifestation of sexism; the belief that men = “humanity”. Putting the masculine point of view at the center of everything, diminishing femininity.

    Sexism

    Mental structure/social construct in which a rigid gender binary is enforced; results in the subordination of women and gender minorities.

    Patriarchy

    Organizational structure of most of the world in which men wield power over women.

    Innocence & Purity

    Innocence is the untaintedness of a woman in terms of sexual activity. Purity is chastity and moral cleanliness, in which a woman’s value is tied to her sexuality. The Blessed Mother Mary is considered innocent and pure due to her lack of sexual activity and her virginity, perpetuating an unattainable value for women in Catholicism.

    Moral Justice

    Understanding that one can’t make good choices without a good set of facts; overcoming of gaps of knowledge in order to make good decisions.

    Social Justice

    Justice with an aim to create a world one wants to live in, which is anti-oppressive and is relation-centered instead of act-centered.

    Gender Binary

    The strict societal bounds in which there are only two genders that must abide by a set of strict gender norms within society and culture.

    Social Sin

    Systemic injustices embedded in society that go beyond individual actions.
